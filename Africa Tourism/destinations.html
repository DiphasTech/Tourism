Africa Awaits

Our Destinations

Six Extraordinary Places

Landscapes That Change You

Each destination we offer has been explored personally by our team — not simply added to a brochure. We know the back roads, the best camps, and the quiet moments that make a journey truly extraordinary.

Tanzania

Serengeti National Park

Home to the Great Wildebeest Migration — two million animals following ancient instincts across 30,000 sq km of pristine savanna. Predator sightings are almost guaranteed year-round.

🗺 Northern Tanzania ⭐ UNESCO World Heritage
🌤 Best: June – October

Botswana

Okavango Delta

One of Africa's most magical ecosystems — a vast inland delta where crystal channels weave between palm islands and papyrus beds alive with hippos, leopards, and over 500 bird species.

🗺 North-West Botswana ⭐ UNESCO World Heritage
🌤 Best: May – September

Uganda

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

Trek through ancient mist-draped jungle to encounter mountain gorillas — half the world's entire population lives here. A deeply humbling, unforgettable experience.

🗺 South-West Uganda ⭐ UNESCO World Heritage
🌤 Best: June – August & Dec – Feb

Kenya

Masai Mara Reserve

Kenya's crown jewel — dense with the Big Five and sharing the great migration with Tanzania across the Mara River. Rise at dawn for a balloon ride above amber grasslands.

🗺 Rift Valley, Kenya ⭐ Africa's Top Reserve
🌤 Best: July – October

Rwanda

Volcanoes National Park

The land of a thousand hills — track gorillas through volcanic forests, hike the Virunga chain, and discover golden monkeys in misty bamboo groves.

🗺 North-West Rwanda ⭐ Premier Primate Destination
🌤 Best: June – September

Zimbabwe

Victoria Falls

The Smoke That Thunders — one of the world's largest waterfalls, with adventure options from white-water rafting and bungee jumping to game drives in Hwange National Park.

🗺 Western Zimbabwe ⭐ UNESCO World Heritage
🌤 Best: February – May
